My LeetCode Problem Solutions

This is a repository containing all of the solutions I have submitted to the LeetCode site. They are not necessarily the best possible solutions and may have optimizations that could be made to achieve a better result, but they all pass with correct output. This document contains a list of all entries with an indicator of whether or not they pass all test cases. The entries on the LeetCode website are split based on difficulty, and that is how they will be classified here as well. I will also give my own assessment of difficulty.

Difficulty Scale

I will be using a scale from 1 to 10 to give my own rating of difficulty. They will roughly correspond to these levels:
  • 1/10-4/10 - Perfectly reasonable to use for coding tests in job interviews, even short ones - trivial tasks, easier stuff, rated mostly on expected time to spend writing it
  • 5/10 - More work, reasonable for up to a 1 hour coding test.
  • 6/10 - Still reasonable for a 1-2 hour test.
  • 7/10 - Reasonable for a take-home type of coding test, not really suitable for a live interview coding test.
  • 8/10 - Give the person taking the test at least a day.
  • 9/10 - Give them 2-3 days.
  • 10/10 - If this isn't directly tied to work that will be performed, don't use these.
With any of these problems, I strongly recommend, when using them for assessing candidates for employment, that you either choose challenges that are applicable to most software development tasks or that you pick ones that are directly relevant to the specific job for which candidates are being hired.
